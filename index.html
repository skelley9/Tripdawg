Build your trip profile

Step 1 of 10

Where are you starting from?

This does not need to be perfect. It helps TripDawg prioritize driveable options vs. fly-to options.

What kind of group is going?

This is about logistics and vibe, not labels. Pick the closest fit.

How many people are going?

Group size affects lodging recommendations, cost estimates and whether walkability matters more.

How far are you willing to go?

This is the main travel constraint. It prevents the app from recommending a cross-country flight when you wanted a weekend drive.

How long is the trip?

TripDawg will build the itinerary length around this.

What time of year?

Season matters a lot for parks, beaches, desert trips, ski trips and crowds.

What do you actually want to do?

Pick everything that matters. If nothing matters, hit no preference.

What vibe should the trip have?

Pick one primary vibe. This keeps the results from fighting themselves.

What is the budget per person?

This is a rough planning budget for lodging, travel, food, activities and misc. spending.

Any lodging preferences or dealbreakers?

Both are optional. These help avoid obviously bad matches.
